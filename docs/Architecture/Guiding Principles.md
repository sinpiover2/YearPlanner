# Guiding Principles

These principles are the DNA of Year Planner.

They are not implementation details.

They are decision filters.

---

# The Organizing Question

> Am I OK?

Everything in the application exists to help answer that question.

---

# Information Order

Reality

?

Consequence

?

Recommendation

Never reverse the order.

Facts create trust.

---

# Timeline = Orientation

The timeline answers:

> Where am I?

It does not answer:

> Should I care?

Cards answer that question.

---

# Cards = Interpretation

Cards answer:

- Should I care?
- What happens if nothing changes?
- Can I fix this?

---

# Drift Is Geometric

Teachers understand position more naturally than numbers.

The distance matters more than the variance.

---

# The Timeline Behaves Like a Map

Rows stay.

The year stays.

The teacher moves.

Position is more important than numbers.

---

# Periods Are Landmarks

Rows stay.

Synchronization changes interpretation, not geometry.

Stable structures reduce cognitive load.

---

# Breaks Are Terrain

Breaks are part of the year itself.

They are not decorations.

---

# Compression Is Kindness

Complexity should be compressed whenever possible.

But compression should never hide information.

---

# Stability Is Kindness

Teachers should not have to relearn the interface.

Favor stable geometry over clever geometry.

---

# Semantic Colors Convey Meaning

Green:

On Track

Amber:

Monitoring and Needs Attention

Red:

Buffer Exhausted

Red is reserved.

---

# Course Colors Convey Identity

Course colors are independent of status colors.

Identity and meaning are different.

---

# Progress Uses Area

Light background:

Planned

Dark fill:

Completed

Area communicates progress more naturally than icons.

---

# Most Teachers Are Fine Most of the Time

The default emotional state is calm.

Warnings should be rare and meaningful.

---

# The Best Designs Disappear

Every major improvement so far has come from removing something.

Invisible design is successful design.

---

# Treat Teachers as Professionals

Year Planner provides clarity.

Teachers provide judgment.

The software should support decisions, not make them.
