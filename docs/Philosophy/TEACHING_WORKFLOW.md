# Teaching Workflow

## Purpose

This document describes the work of teaching.

It intentionally avoids discussing software.

Software should emerge from this workflow rather than define it.

---

---

# Prepare

Goal:

Reduce uncertainty before students arrive.

Questions:

What am I teaching?

What materials do I need?

What should students learn?

What should they turn in?

Where might they struggle?

Output:

A lesson ready to teach.

---

# Teach

Goal:

Pay attention to students.

Not the computer.

The lesson plan exists to reduce thinking during instruction, not demand attention.

During teaching the teacher primarily thinks about:

¥ student understanding

¥ pacing

¥ misconceptions

¥ engagement

¥ time

---

# Reflect

Reflection happens immediately after teaching whenever possible.

Questions:

What happened?

What surprised me?

What should I remember?

What should I change next year?

Reflection creates teaching wisdom.

---

# Improve

Reflection may improve

¥ Teaching Episodes

¥ Curriculum notes

¥ Personal teaching wisdom

These are different.

---

# The teacher's questions

Teachers rarely ask:

"What document did I create?"

Teachers ask:

"What happened Tuesday?"

"What do students need to turn in?"

"What should they have learned?"

"What do I need tomorrow?"

The software should answer teacher questions rather than expose database objects.

---

# The software's responsibility

Help the teacher think.

Remember.

Organize.

Reduce unnecessary mental effort.

Never compete with students for attention.
