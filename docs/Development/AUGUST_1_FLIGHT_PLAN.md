# August 1 Flight Plan

> "Can Jeff teach every day without wishing he were back in FileMaker?"

---

# Purpose

This document governs every development decision between now and August 1.

It is not a feature roadmap.

It is a decision filter.

Every proposed feature, sprint, architectural decision, and UI improvement should be evaluated against this document.

The objective is simple:

> Replace FileMaker as Jeff's trusted daily teaching workspace.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

---

# Mission

By August 1, Year Planner must be capable of supporting daily teaching without requiring FileMaker.

Success is measured by one question:

> **At the end of the first month of school, has Jeff stopped opening FileMaker because Year Planner is now his trusted workspace?**

If yes, Version 1 is successful.

---

# Success Criteria

By August 1 Jeff should be able to:

- Plan an instructional week.
- Prepare tomorrow's lessons.
- Print lesson plans.
- Teach from the printed lesson.
- Record retrieval cues during instruction.
- Return the next day fully prepared.

Without opening FileMaker.

---

# The Product Goal

Year Planner should help Jeff become:

1. Calmer
2. More organized
3. More effective

Always in that order.

Reducing anxiety creates space for organization.

Organization creates space for better teaching.

Better teaching improves student learning.

---

# Daily Cognitive Workflow

The software exists to support the teacher's thinking.

Different kinds of thinking require different interfaces.

---

## Before School

### Questions

- Am I OK?
- What deserves my attention?

### Responsibilities

Year Planner should:

- Show Forecast confidence.
- Confirm today's schedule.
- Make today's lessons immediately available.
- Support printing.
- Surface only meaningful concerns.
- Eliminate uncertainty.

### Success

Within one minute Jeff can:

- Open Year Planner.
- Know he is ready.
- Print today's lessons.
- Walk to the copier.

Morning is execution preparation, not planning.

---

## During Teaching

### Questions

None.

Teaching is live.

### Responsibilities

Teacher:

- Observe.
- Listen.
- Adapt.
- Teach.

Clipboard:

- Retrieval cues.
- Quick marks.
- Circles.
- Arrows.
- Trigger words.

Year Planner:

Nothing.

The software has already completed its job.

### Success

The teacher forgets the software and is fully present with students.

---

## First Safe Eddy

The first calm moment after teaching.

This may be:

- Prep period.
- Passing period.
- Lunch.
- Immediately after school.

### Questions

What happened?

### Responsibilities

Teacher:

Narrate naturally.

AI (Future):

- Preserve.
- Organize.
- Structure.
- Connect.

### Success

The experience is reconstructed before memory fades.

---

## End of Day

### Questions

- What happened?
- What still needs attention?
- Am I ready for tomorrow?

### Success

Jeff leaves school feeling organized instead of mentally carrying unfinished work home.

---

## Friday Afternoon

Questions:

- What happened this week?
- What did I learn?
- What should change next week?

Planning comes after learning.

# Product Principles

## Technology follows cognition.

The interface emerges from the teacher's thinking.

Never the reverse.

---

## Trust before intelligence.

The workspace must earn trust before AI earns trust.

---

## Manage Teaching

?

Support Teaching

?

Develop Teachers

These layers are built in order.

Never the reverse.

---

## Planning happens before teaching.

Reflection happens after teaching.

Teaching belongs entirely to students.

---

## During teaching, software disappears.

The lesson is live.

The clipboard becomes the live system.

Technology returns only after the teacher reaches a safe eddy.

---

## Preserve retrieval cues, not documentation.

The objective is not to capture everything.

The objective is to preserve enough to reconstruct the experience later.

---

## Optimize for teacher energy.

Energy

?

Attention

?

Thinking

?

Teaching Decisions

?

Student Learning

Time savings matter only if they ultimately preserve teacher energy.

---

## Good software makes the next decision obvious.

Like a good mathematical visualization, the software should organize reality so well that the appropriate next action naturally emerges.

It should not tell the teacher what to think.

---

## Planning creates freedom.

Planning is not intended to force adherence to a lesson.

Planning provides the confidence to depart from the lesson intentionally when students need something different.

Forecast supports professional judgment.

It never replaces it.

---

# Definition of Done

Version 1 is complete when:

- Jeff naturally opens Year Planner each morning.
- Jeff naturally prints from Year Planner.
- Jeff teaches from the printed lesson.
- Jeff no longer opens FileMaker.
- Jeff trusts the workspace.
- Jeff finishes the day calmer than he started.

At that point, Version 2 begins.

Only then does the focus shift from managing teaching to accelerating professional growth.
